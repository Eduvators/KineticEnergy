Kinetic Energy Transformation

In electrical circuits, loads consume electrical energy and convert it into various forms of energy. One important transformation is the conversion to kinetic energy (motion).

Kinetic Energy

Electrical energy can be transformed into motion or kinetic energy through motors and other devices.

Example: In electric motors, when electricity powers the motor, it creates a magnetic field that interacts with magnets, causing rotational motion. This converts electrical energy into kinetic energy, which provides traction to the vehicle and powers auxiliary components such as wiper motors, power windows, and cooling fans.
